What AI Video Generation Costs: Runway vs Luma AI

The first three pieces in this series covered how AI services break down by category, how to pick a coding assistant plan, and how Midjourney's billing works. This one covers AI video generation — Runway and Luma AI are the two most common tools, but they bill completely differently: Runway draws down compute credits by generated seconds, while Luma AI runs on a subscription-plus-credits model. This breaks down how to estimate real cost, pick a tier, and pay for either with the same setup.

ipfsWeb · AI Access Guide Editorial · Published 2026-07-23 13:34:38

1. AI video generation bills on more dimensions than image generation

Midjourney's billing centers on one variable — Fast hours. AI video tools usually stack several at once: clip length in seconds, resolution, and generation mode (standard vs high-quality) all move the actual charge. Runway runs on a "compute credit" system, where each generation draws credits based on duration and quality settings; Luma AI starts as a subscription with a set number of generations or credits included, and charges separately once you exceed it. Miss this and it's easy to burn through credits far faster than expected after just a handful of long, high-resolution clips.

3. How to estimate what you'll actually pay

Neither tool works as "subscription fee = total cost" — actual spend depends on usage intensity. The estimation method is simple: check how many credits or generations the plan includes, then work backward from how many clips, how long, and at what resolution you plan to generate. If you regularly need 10-second-plus clips at high resolution, credits burn much faster than the default assumption, and a higher tier can end up cheaper than repeated top-ups. Run the entry tier for a week or two first, watch how fast credits actually deplete, then decide whether to upgrade.

4. Which tier fits an individual vs a team

If you only generate a handful of short clips occasionally for social posts, the entry tier is usually enough, and topping up credits occasionally won't sting. If you create content consistently, edit effects, or iterate frequently, a higher tier's larger credit allowance avoids constantly triggering overage purchases. For teams, both Runway and Luma AI bill by subscription account rather than strictly per head, so the planning question is the same as with Midjourney — "how large a credit tier do we need," not "how many accounts do we buy." The team-allocation approach from our Picking a Midjourney Plan: How GPU Time Billing Works applies here too.

5. Paying with stablecoin or a virtual card

Runway and Luma AI bill the same way other overseas AI services do — through a card or whatever other channel the platform supports. If you're already paying for ChatGPT or Midjourney with a virtual card or stablecoins, the same setup carries over directly to video generation tools; no separate payment channel needed. 6. Common pitfalls

A few issues come up often enough to plan for: long, high-resolution generations burn credits far faster than the default assumption — two "one video" generations can cost very different amounts depending on parameters, so check the estimated cost before generating. Credits on some tiers don't roll over month to month and reset to zero unused, so matching your subscription to actual need beats stockpiling. Teams sharing one account without an agreed usage rhythm often burn through credits early in the month and run short by the end — reserve allowance by project instead of first-come-first-served.

7. Summary

Runway bills compute credits by seconds and quality; Luma AI runs a subscription-plus-credits model. Neither is a simple flat fee, so run the entry tier first and watch actual burn rate before upgrading rather than buying the top tier up front. Payment still comes down to the same virtual card or stablecoin setup as any other subscription.
