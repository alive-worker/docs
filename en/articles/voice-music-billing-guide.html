ElevenLabs vs Suno: AI Voice and Music Subscription Costs

Earlier pieces covered coding assistants, AI image generation, and AI video generation — three categories of advanced AI tools with their own billing logic. This one covers sound: ElevenLabs is the most widely used AI voice and voice-cloning tool, while Suno is the leading text-to-music product. Their billing differs from everything covered so far: ElevenLabs charges directly by the character count of the text you convert to speech, while Suno runs on a subscription plan plus a generation count. This breaks down how to pick a tier for either, common pitfalls, and how to pay for both with the same setup.

ipfsWeb · AI Access Guide Editorial · Published 2026-07-23 18:01:04

1. AI voice and music generation bill on completely different units

ElevenLabs bills on "character count" — however much text you convert to speech is what you draw down, largely independent of the resulting audio's length or how complex the voice is; it mainly comes down to text length. Suno works closer to Midjourney's model: a subscription plan includes a set number of generations (credits), each song generation draws down a fixed number of credits, and once you run out you either wait for the next cycle or buy more. These two billing models require completely different usage estimates: for ElevenLabs, work out how much text you'll convert before subscribing; for Suno, figure out roughly how many songs you'll generate per month.

3. Voice cloning and commercial licensing are ElevenLabs' extra gate

ElevenLabs' voice cloning feature (training a custom voice model from a recording) is usually gated to mid-to-high tiers, and different tiers carry explicit limits on how many cloned voices you can create and how far the commercial license extends — free or entry tiers often only let you use the platform's stock voices, not upload your own for cloning. If your use case is "generate voiceovers at scale in my own voice," confirm the voice-cloning permissions and commercial terms before picking a tier, rather than discovering the feature is locked to a higher tier after paying.

4. Which tier fits an individual creator

If you only generate a handful of voiceover clips occasionally for video narration, ElevenLabs' entry tier is usually enough. If you're doing ongoing podcast or audiobook work — long-form text-to-speech — characters burn through faster than expected, so estimate the actual character count from one episode before working out the tier you need. On Suno's side, occasional background music is fine on the base subscription; if you're consistently producing short-video scoring or music content, a higher generation allowance avoids repeatedly hitting "used up for the month."

5. Paying with stablecoin or a virtual card

ElevenLabs and Suno bill the same way other overseas AI services do — through a card or whatever other channel the platform supports. If you're already paying for ChatGPT or Midjourney with a virtual card or stablecoins, the same setup carries over directly to voice and music generation tools; no separate payment channel needed. 6. Common pitfalls

A few issues come up often enough to plan for: ElevenLabs' character billing doesn't distinguish between languages, so the actual cost of converting non-English text doesn't map cleanly onto a per-word estimate — test a short passage first to check the real unit cost before converting a large batch. Suno's generation credits don't roll over month to month and reset unused, so matching your subscription to actual need beats stockpiling. Commercial licensing terms — especially around voice cloning and the copyright scope of generated music — vary by tier, so check the official terms carefully before using either for a commercial project to avoid infringement risk.

7. Summary

ElevenLabs bills by character count, Suno runs a subscription-plus-generations model — neither is a simple flat fee, so estimating actual usage before picking a tier beats buying the top tier upfront. Extra gates like voice cloning and commercial licensing are worth confirming before you pay. Payment still comes down to the same virtual card or stablecoin setup as any other subscription.
